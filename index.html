THE UNITED REPUBLIC OF TANZANIA
WELCOME TO PUBLIC SERVICE SOCIAL SECURITY FUND (PSSSF)

Public Service Social Security Fund (PSSSF) was established on August 1st, 2018 under the Public Service Social Security Fund Act (PSSSF Act) of 2018 that provides for contribution to and payments of social security benefits in respect of the service of employees in the public service. The fund helps to keep the contributions of their members and help them to live a better life after retirement in the old age. The fund provides some of the following benefits.

Old age Pension:

This is the regular amount of money that people receive from the fund when they have retired from work.

Conditions for Old age Pension

(a) A member shall have contributed to the Fund for the period of not less than 180 months or 15 years.

(b) A member shall attain the retirement age.

Formula used for calculation this type of benefit is: -

(i) Commuted Pension Gratuity = 1/580 * Months of Service * Average highest Salary of 36 Months * 0.33 * 12.5

(ii) Monthly Pension = 1/580 * Months of Service * Average Highest Salary of 36 Months * 0.67 / 12

Survivor Pension:

This is the benefit paid to dependents of a deceased member whose contribution credit is 180 months or more.

Conditions for a member to qualify for this benefit are:

(a) A deceased member has contributed to the Fund

(b) For dependents to be paid survivor benefit, a deceased member must have contributed to the Fund for the period of at least 180 months.

(c) If the deceased member has not contributed to the Fund for at least 180 months, dependents shall not be eligible for survivor benefit.

(d) Those who are entitled for this benefit are widow or widower, children under 21 years and parents if there is no defendant spouse or children.

Invalidity Pension:

This is the social insurance payment that may be paid to people who cannot work because of a long term illness or disability.

Conditions for a member to qualify for this benefit are:

(a) A member has been terminated from employment due to physical or mental illness, which is not work-related sickness or accident.

(b) A member is below the age of voluntarily retirement

(c) A member has contributed to the scheme for at least 36 months of which, 12 months have been contributed in the year preceding the illness.

Formula used for calculation this type of benefit is:

(i) Invalidity Benefit = 1/580 * Months of Service * Average highest Salary of 36 Months * 0.33 * 12.5

Sickness benefit:

Is the money paid by the fund to a person who is too sick to work. It's paid to a member who is suffering from sickness or accident not resulting from employment-related injury for more than 3 months and due to that illness, he is being paid half salary.
